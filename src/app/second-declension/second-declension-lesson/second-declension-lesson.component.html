Accentuation of first declension substantives and adjectives

The accentuation of first declension substantives and adjectives observes the first main rule:

The accent follows the nominative singular as much as possible. (MR1)

Note that this is not always possible in light of the limitation rule.

Special rules

  1. Oxytones become perispomena in the gen./dat. (sg./du./pl.): κακός, κακῷ, κακοῖν, κακοῖς.
    • Exception: substantives and adjectives of the Attic declension keep their nom.sg. accentuation in the gen.pl.: ὁ λεώς, τοῦ λεώ, τῶν λεών.
  2. Contracted oxytones in -οῦς are persipomenon in all cases apart from the nom.-acc.du.: ὁ νοῦς, τώ νώ.

Desinential lengths

  • Nom.pl. -οι is always short: nom.pl. οἶκοι 'houses'.
  • The locative ending -οι is long: οἴκοι.
