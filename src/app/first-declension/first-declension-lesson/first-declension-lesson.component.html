Accentuation of first declension substantives and adjectives

The accentuation of first declension substantives and adjectives observes the first main rule:

The accent follows the nominative singular as much as possible. (MR1)

Note that this is not always possible in light of the limitation rule.

Special rules

  1. Oxytones become perispomena in the gen./dat. (sg./du./pl.): ψυχή, ψυχῆς, ψυχῇ, ψυχήν, ψυχά, ψυχαῖν (FD1).
  2. The genitive plural is always perispomenon due to contraction: μοῦσα > (μουσάων >) μουσῶν (FD2)
    • Exception: the genitive plural of adjectives follows the accentuation of the nominative singular if the stem is the same as that of the masculine and neuter: ἄξιος > ἄξίων (m./f./n.), but μέλας > gen.pl.f. μελαινῶν (FD2), but m./n. μελάνων (MR1)

Desinential lengths

  • Nom./acc.sg. -α(ν) in substantives can be long -ᾱ(ν) (< PIE *-eh2(m)) or short -ᾰ(ν) (< PIE *-h2(m)). In adjectives, -ᾱ(ν) is always long (because of the ρει-rule).
  • Acc.pl. -ᾱς is always long in Ionic-Attic (because of the Second Compensatory Lengthening).
  • Nom.pl. -ᾰι is always short.
  • Voc.sg. -ᾰ is always short in masculines in -ης and -της: ναύτης > ναῦτᾰ.

Advanced material

  1. The Ionic genitive in -εω is considered one syllable and does not force an accent shift: Πηληϊάδης > Πηληϊάδεω (**Πηληϊαδέω), Ἑρμῆς > Ἑρμἐω. (FD+1)
  2. Genitives in -ιω, -ια are paroxytone: ἐϋμμελίω, Ἐρμείω, ἐϋμμελία. (FD+2)
  3. Genitives in -αο are recessive: Ἀτρεΐδης > Ἀτρείδου / Ἀτρείδαο, ἀργεστής > ἀργεστοῦ / ἀργεστᾶο. (FD+3)
