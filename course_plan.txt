
- General introduction // Nature of the accent
	- Ancient Greek was a tonal language.
	- Differences in pitch can indicate differences in meaning.
	...inherited from PIE
	
	- Accent Marks		
		- There are three Greek accent marks: acute, grave and circumflex.
		- These marks are written on top of vowel signs.
			- The acute indicates a high tone before a drop.(?)
			- The perispomenon indicates a high tone and a drop on the same long vowel.
			- The grave accent (ὰ) is a variant of the acute if it falls on the ultimate when another accented word follows.
				* The acute remains on τίς, τί.
				* The acute remains in pauses: before interpunction marks and verse ends.
	
		- Generally, an accented (orthotonic) Greek word only carries one accent mark.
		- Some words are inherently unaccented (barytone): proclitic ἐν and enclitic τις.
		- In combination with clitics, a single word may carry two accents: ἄνθρωπός τις.
	
	
- Course structure

	- Chapter 1: General principles
		- Vowel length and the accent:
			- The acute (ά) and grave (ὰ) are found on both long and short vowels.
			- The circumflex (ᾶ) is only found on long vowels and diphthongs.		
			- Always short are ε / ο. 
			- Always long are η / ω and the diphthongs ει, ου, ωυ, ῃ etc.
			- The vowels α / ι / υ can be either long or short and their quantity has to be learned (though guides will be given in this app).
			- The diphthongs αι and οι are short in the last syllable.
				* The optative -αι -οι and locative -οι are long.
				
			Exercises
	
		- Limitation Law: the accent cannot stand further back than the antepenult.
			- The acute (ά) can stand on the ultimate, the penult and the antepenult.
			- The circumflex (ᾶ) can stand on the penult and the ultimate.
			(- The grave (ὰ) can only stand on the ultimate.)
			- The accent cannot fall on the antepenult if the ultimate contains a long vowel, diphthong or ends in two consonants: -ψ, -ξ: φιλόλογος - φιλοκόλαξ, ἄνθρωπος, ἀνθρώπου.
				* Compounds with enclitics: ὤντινων.
				* Metathesis quantitatis: 
					- ἵλεως, Μενέλεως (< Μενέλᾱος)
					- Gen.sg. of ι/υ-stems: πόλεως
					- Some compound adjectives in -ως: δύσερως, ὑψίκερως
				
			- The accent may shift because of this: ἄνθρωπος > ἀνθρώπου, βελτίων > βέλτιον
			- This leads to a limited number of tonal patterns:
				- Oxytone
				- Paroxytone
				- Proparoxytone
				- Perispomenon
				- Properispomenon
				- Barytone (= toneless)
			Exercises:
				- Shift the accent only if necessary:
					ἄνθρωπος > ἄνθρωπον / ἀνθρώπου
				- What is the quantity of the following vowels
					θάλαττα, ἥμισυς, πῆχυς, δύναμις, μῆνις, ὀφρῦς, φίλος
		
		
		
		- Σωτῆρα Rule: an accent on a long penult with short ultimate following must be a perispomenon: σωτήρ > σωτῆρα, δοῦλος, ναῦται.
			* Compounds with enclitics do not follow this rule: οἴδε, οὔτις, ὥστε.
	
	- Chapter 2: Contraction
		- When two vowels contract, the following accent rules take place:
			- Unaccented + unaccented => unaccented
			- Unaccented + accented: acute: ἐποιε-όμην => ἐποιούμην
			- Accented + unaccented: circumflex: ἐποιέ-ομεν => ἐποιοῦμεν
		Exercise: contraction verbs of νικάω, ποιέω, δηλόω (all recessive)

	- Chapter 4: Nouns (substantives and adjectives)
		- Main rule: accent stays where it is in the nominative singular.
			
		- First Declension
			- Main rule is followed.
				(- Perispomena stay perispomena: Ἀθηνᾶ, Ἀθηνᾶς, Ἀθηνᾷ, Ἀθηνᾶν.)
			
			FD1 Oxytones become perispomena in the gen./dat. (sg./du./pl.): 
					=> ψυχή, ψυχῆς, ψυχῇ, ψυχήν, ψυχά, ψυχαῖν.
			
			FD2 As a result: gen.pl. => always perispomenon in substantives
				μοῦσα > μουσάων > μουσῶν.
			
			
			Bonus: Ionic genitive in -εω is considered as one syllable:
				Πηληϊάδης > Πηληϊάδεω
				Ἑρμῆς > Ἑρμέω
			Bonus: genitives in -ιω, -ειω, -ια are paroxytone
				ἐϋμμελίω, Ἐρμείω, ἐϋμμελία
			Bonus: genitives in -αο are recessive
				Ἀτρεΐδης > Ἀτρείδου / Ἀτρείδαο
				ἀργεστής > ἀργεστοῦ / ἀργεστᾶο
			
			Adjectives follow the main rule: nom.sg.m.
				=> Genitives are perispomenon if their stem is different from the masc./ntr.; else they will follow the masc./ntr. (columnar)
					ἄξιος > m./f./n. ἀξίων
					μέλας > m./n. μελάνων, f. μελαινῶν
				
			Ambiguous vowels:
			- Adjectives: nom.sg. -α is always long): *-eh2
				=> this is because of the REI-rule
				=> Substantives: *-eh2 / -h2
			- Acc.pl. -ας is always long (but not in Doric)
				=> this is because of the 2nd CV
			- Nom.pl. -αι is always short
			- Acc.sg. => -αν can be long or short
				=> Actually: 
			(- Vocative sg. => -α is always short in masculines in -ης, -της)
				=> *-h2
